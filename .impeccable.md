## Design Context

### Users
Casual to intermediate blackjack players who want to improve their basic strategy. They may be learning the ropes or tightening a few weak spots in their game. Mobile use spans quick commute sessions and focused practice — the full feature set should be accessible on both.

### Brand Personality
Sharp. Luxurious. Focused. The experience should feel like a premium casino app, not a classroom. No gamification gimmicks, no cartoonish energy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Deep casino green felt, warm gold accents, cream text. Playfair Display for display headings, Inter for UI. Dark-only. The mobile design is a direct adaptation of the desktop — same visual language, same components, just optimised for thumb reach and small viewports. Do not drift from the established palette, typography, or surface treatments.

**Anti-reference**: Duolingo — avoid anything that feels gamified, brightly coloured, badge-heavy, or cartoonishly friendly.

### Design Principles
1. **Continuity over reinvention** — mobile should feel like the same app on a smaller screen, not a stripped-down version.
2. **Thumb-first layout** — primary actions (Hit, Stand, Double, Split) should be reachable without stretching.
3. **Density without clutter** — compress vertical rhythm on small screens; don't hide content, tighten the spacing.
4. **Touch-ready surfaces** — all interactive targets 44×44px minimum; generous tap areas on buttons.
5. **No feature amputation** — every mode and panel available on desktop stays available on mobile, just re-arranged.
